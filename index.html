How does technology
shape us as moral beings?

Hi! I'm Minha, an assistant professor
at the Eindhoven University of Technology's
Industrial Design department.

I explore how we can design interactions
with artificial agents like robots or chatbots
for exploring our sense of
moral concepts such as compassion or fairness.
If technology extends who we are,
what does it mean to treat AI compassionately or fairly?
About me

Currently, my interest in moral emotions centers on compassion and how it can help with mental well-being. Here is my presentation on Vincent, a chatbot for self-compassion. I am curious about other moral emotions like gratitude, guilt, or awe, and if technology can be incorporated to explore them.

I'm a Korean citizen, humbled by Minnesotan winters. Previously, I studied Human Centered Multimedia at the University of Amsterdam, Philosophy at the University of Minnesota - Twin Cities, and Digital Arts at Pratt Intitute where I created works like an animated short that was apparently banned by a festival. Other information can be found on my CV.

I am affiliated with the Center for Humans and Technology of the TU/e and the 4TU research center Humans and Technology for continuous collaboration between four technical universities of the Netherlands. My home base is Atlas (pictured here) where the Industrial Design department is located. Check out our latest research.
